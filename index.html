L'architecture française : un voyage à travers le temps

Églises françaises à travers le temps

Style roman (Xe–XIIe siècle)

Murs épais en pierre, petites fenêtres et arcs en plein cintre, leur donnant un aspect solide et robuste. Exemples : Basilique Saint-Sernin, Cathédrale d'Autun

Style gothique (XIIe–XVIe siècle)

Arcs en ogive élevés, vitraux colorés. Exemples : Notre-Dame de Paris, Cathédrale de Chartres.

Renaissance et Baroque (XVIe–XVIIIe siècle)

Renaissance churches had classical elements like domes and symmetrical designs, while Baroque churches had grand decorations and curved shapes. Example: Église Saint-Gervais-Saint-Protais.

Modern Churches (19th–20th Century)

The 19th century brought back the Gothic style in some churches, while modern designs used new materials and creative shapes, like Notre-Dame du Haut by Le Corbusier.

French Castles and Palaces

Medieval Castles

These castles were built for defense, with thick walls, towers, and moats. Examples: Château de Carcassonne, Château de Vincennes.

Fancy Palaces

As times became more peaceful, castles became luxurious palaces. They had elegant decorations and beautiful gardens. Examples: Château de Chambord, Versailles.

French Gardens

Renaissance and Baroque Gardens

These gardens became more decorative, with fountains, terraces, and trimmed hedges. The Jardin du Luxembourg in Paris is a great example.

Classical French Gardens

Perfect symmetry, fountains, and trimmed hedges. Example: Versailles Gardens.

Modern Gardens

Today’s gardens focus more on nature and sustainability, mixing classic beauty with eco-friendly designs, like Parc André Citroën.
